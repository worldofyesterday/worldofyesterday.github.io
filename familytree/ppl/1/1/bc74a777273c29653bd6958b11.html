Narrative

Ethnomusicologist. In "Report of the fourth congress of the international musical society (London 1911), his address is 15 Arenbergring, Vienna. This is where father Wilhelm (Wolf) was living at time of his death in 1928 (JOWBR).

Narrative

From Jeremy LEONG:

ca. 1900 -- ca. 1929 : Active in Vienna. Enrolled as a student at the Imperial Academy of Science and received his Ph.D. in Music with a dissertaion entitled "Indian Music from the Vedic and the Classical Periods" (1912) under the supervision of Prof. Leopold Shroder. His book on Indian music (which was likely to be a revision of his dissertation) was published in Vienna and he was a well-known scholar on Indian music which he'd given many lectures at the "Indian Club" in Vienna. He held the position of collaborator and music advisor of the record archives of the Imperial Academy of Science (Vienna). By dating backward based on his later photo (see attachment), I surmise that he was probably in his mid 20s to early 30s when he received his Ph.D. in 1912.

ca. mid 1930s -- 1949 : When the Nazis took over Germany and later Austria, Jews began to migrate abroad and about 18,000 of them (mainly from Germany and Austria) ended up in Shanghai (and some even went south to the province of Fujian). The number of Jewish immigrants in Shanghai peaked in 1939 and the reason why so many of them landed in China was because the metropolis (then controlled by the Japanese) required no immigration documents nor background check (the U.S., Canada, Australia, all have immigration requirements and quotas). Many Austro-German Jews came with very little belongings (the Third Reich only allowed 1 suitcase and 10 marks per person leaving the country) and carrying proper documentation was unlikely to be the top of their priorities. Felber arrived in Shanghai probably in 1938 and became the artistic director for the European Jewish Artists Society, established in 1939 to preserve music culture of Jewish refugees from Europe. He was also a music lecturer at Asia Seminar, an advanced institution established in 1943 to provide adult Jews the opportunity to gain a comprehensive education in Shanghai. He also taught Western music at a local university (Shanghai University).
