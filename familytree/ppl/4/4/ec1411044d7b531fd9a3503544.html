                         Curriculum vitae.

                     I was born in 1892 in Vienna (former Austria, now Germany) and went there to primary and secondary school. In 1910 I passed matriculation and from 1910 to 1914 I attended lectures at Vienna University, studying Physics, Mathematics and Chemistry. My teachers were: V.v.Lang (Physics), E.Lecher (Physics), F.Hasenöhrl (Physics), F.Ehrehaft (Physics), E.v.Schweidler(Physics), F.Exner(Physics), H.Thirring (Physics), E.Schrödinger (Physics), W.Wirtinger (Mathematics), F.Mertens (Mathematics), G.v.Escherich (Mathematics), P.Furtwängler(Mathematica), H.Hahn (Mathematics), G.Goldschmid (Chemics), K.Schlenk (Chemics), R.Wegscheider (Chemics),St.Meyer (Radioactivity), K.Pribram (Radioactivity).

                      At the same time I started with Research work already but had not yet completed my first paper when the World War broke out. As a soldier I continued with researsh work and got a classification as an examiner of inventions for the War Office. In 1916 I gained the degree of a Ph.D. (Physics and Mathematics). After the war I worked again at Vienna University and published about 10 papers on various physical questions. I got then calls f.i. as a professor of the University of Constantinople (Turkey) and of an American University, further as the head of a technical and physical laboratory of a cable factory in Vienna and at the same time of the Cable Manufacturing Co.,in Bratislava, Czechoslovakia.

                      I finally decided for the latter and joined that factory in the beginning of 1922. At the same time I was appointed a Privatdozent (something like an Assistant Professor) for Physics in general at Vienna University.

                      At the Cable Manufacturing Co.,Bratislava I founded the Physical and Electrical Testing and Research Laboratory there and, since at that time practically nothing of value existed there, I had  to design not only the laboratory and its further development but also a number of apparatus too. I had to train the whole staff of the laboratory from its beginning.

                      In 1922 I was aware of the big advantages of the so called H-type cables which were not yet common known at that time end our factory was one of the first European to manufacture and lay them. We were also among the first companies who undertook to measure the dieletric losses of laid cables.

                      At Vienna University I had (besides lectures on pure physics)  lectures on physical and electrical problems of Cable Engineering and of Electrical Insulating Material and I suppose that these were the first lectures of that kind held at an European High School. An illustration
         of the subjects I taught at Vienna University is given in annex I.

                      I had occasion to visit several European and American Cable Factories, Factories for Insulating Material and Research or Testing Laboratories (in Italy, Austria, Hungary, Poland, Germany, Switzerland, France, Holland, England, Belgium, Norway, Sweden and USA). Besides I kept in touch with American, Austrian, Dutch, German, Japanese, English, Swedish factories or laboratories or experts of that branch. Gradually I became in charge of all Progress work with the concern of the Cable Manufacturing Co., Bratislava, which had factories for cables and insulating material in Czechoslovakia, Austria, Switzerland, Poland and Hungary.

                      My pupils and myself we published a number of papers on physical problems and/or problems of cables and insulating material in several languages, an excerpt of which is contained in annex II. As far as is known to me my pupils are now working in Czechoslovakia, Germany, Holland,Belgium, Poland and USA.

                      In 1930 I was apppinted Assistant Technical Director and since 1933 I was the only one technical director of the cable mill of the Cable Manufacturing Co., in Bratislava. I left that factory at the beginning of 1939.

                      By my papers, knowledge of languages (English, French, German and Slovak) and by personal contact I have got friends in the industry of cables and insulating materials all over the world. A list of references (academic and technical) is given in annex III.

                      Dr.Kurt Konstantinowsky,
                      10, Crestbrook Place
