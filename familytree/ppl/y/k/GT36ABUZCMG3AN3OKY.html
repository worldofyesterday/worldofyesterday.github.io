Narrative

Mr. and Mrs. William Wilence of 470 West End Ave., New York City, have announced the engagement of their daughter, Marian Edna, to Samuel A. Jacobs, son of Dr. and Mrs. Murray Jacobs of 210 West 90th St, New York. Miss Wilence is a graduate of New York University, a member the the Pi Alpha Tau Sorority, and is employed by the War Department. Mr. Jacobs is a graduate of New York University and a member of the Sigma Epsilon Delta Fraternity. He is a bacteriologist and chemist and is engaged in a research project at the Sheffield Farms plant here.
[Elmira Star Gazette, 18 May 1942, page 14]

Event Note

Paid notices in NY Times (18-Dec-2002)

JACOBS-Samuel A. Shmuel Aaron ben Mordechai, December 16, 2002, after a long illness. Mr. Jacobs raised his family in New York City with his beloved late wife, Marion Wilence Jacobs, and is survived by his sons, Phillip, and Billy, their wives, Marjorie and Lynn, and grandchildren, Elie, Joshua, Michael, Matthew and Benjamin. Mr. Jacobs passed away peacefully in the care of his loving companion, Benita Lebow, to whom he was devoted. Services Wednesday 10 A.M. at ''The Riverside'', 76th Street and Amsterdam Avenue.

JACOBS-Samuel. The officers, Board of Directors, and staff of UJA-Federation of New York mourn the passing of Samuel Jacobs. Mr. Jacobs' son Bill is a member of the Westchester General Campaign Executive Board. Daughter-in-law Lynn Jacobs is a member of the Board of Directors of UJA-Federation. We extend our deepest sympathies to the entire family. Larry Zicklin, President Morris W. Offit, Chair of the Board John S. Ruskay, Exec. V.P. & C.E.O.
